30 Second Film about ROMANTIC LOVE

What is love? a 30-second film exploring romantic love that features a clear epiphany or emotional transition.

Final Cut XElevenLabs

Project

Description

This project explores the paradox of human affection through the lens of color and time. It captures the irony of a thousand days of romantic bond collapsing in just three minutes, shifting the world from a saturated, vibrant landscape into a colorless world. By utilizing a seamless loop, the project illustrates our inevitable return to love, how we knowingly step back into the "coincidence" of a new encounter despite the certain grief that follows. It is a visual testament to the resilient human will to find color again, even after the world turns gray.

Phase Transition of Snow

This project explores the concept of Phase Transition by sonically mapping the physical transformation of snow from a solid to a liquid state through a series of textured stepping sounds. Drawing on Trevor Pinch's "Sculpting Sound" framework, the narrative begins with the high-frequency, crisp "crunch" of dry snow, which gradually dissolves into the dense, low-frequency "slush" of melting ice. The piece culminates in a surreal departure, where the everyday sound of a bus arrival is transformed using Space Designer and volume envelopes into a spaceship-like sonic event.

Computing Art

Examining the collision between "authentically human" elements through computational art.

Reality CaptureBlenderUnity

Project

[ Main media ]

Description

The inspiration for this project began with the observation of a discarded children's slide, an object that represents a profound social and emotional contradiction. While a slide is a universal symbol of childhood joy, its state as "trash" reveals a moment of social abandonment. By digitizing this abandoned object, the project enacts a conversion of lived material reality into a computable form, questioning whether digital preservation can ever truly recover the "aura" of the original experience.

Hack

A project that modifies, augments, or redirects the purpose of an existing object or system.

Arduino

Project

[ Main media ]

Description

This project hacks the most fundamental concept of our environment: silence. In most interactive art, you make noise to get a response. This project, however, hacks that expectation by making silence the active trigger for an alarm. It is an "Inversion Hack"—flipping the rules of cause and effect.
